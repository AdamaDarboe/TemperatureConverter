Converting between Celsius and Fahrenheit involves simple mathematical formulas. To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, you multiply the Celsius temperature by 9/5 and then add 32. For example, to convert 20 degrees Celsius to Fahrenheit: (20°C × 9/5) + 32 = 68°F.

To convert Fahrenheit to Celsius, you subtract 32 from the Fahrenheit temperature and then multiply by 5/9. For example, to convert 68 degrees Fahrenheit to Celsius: (68°F - 32) × 5/9 = 20°C. These conversions are handy for understanding temperature readings in different parts of the world or for scientific calculations.
